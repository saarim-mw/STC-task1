Pastries

Black Forest

The German-inspired pastry became a rage in the UK in the 60s but only caught on in India in the 70s. It consists of layers of chocolate sponge with whipped cream and cherries between the layers.

Sacher Torte

One of the world's great classic pastries, this was invented at the Hotel Sacher in Vienna where it is still the star of the menu. Its a dense chocolate cake with apricot jam inside.

Paris-Brest

It was created to honour a bicycle race from Paris to Brest and the round shape was meant to represent a wheel. It is made of Choux pastry filled with praline cream.

Lamington

The national pastry of Austrailia, is a sponge cake rolled first in chocolate and then in coconut. There is a national Lamington day every July.

Red Velvet

This became famous in the 1990s and also became a common cupcake flavour. It is chiefly notable because of its bright red topping, which does not come from cocoa but is often created using food colour.

Mocha

Depending on your perspective, this is either a chocolate cake layered with coffee buttercream or coffee cake with chocolate flavour.

Chicken Dishes

Tandoori Chicken

Probaby the most famous Indian dish in the world. It was invented in Peshawar in 1930s

Chicken Cafreal

One of the Goan Cuisine's most celebrated dishes. Theories suggest that Portuguese colonists brought it to Goa from Mozambique where it was made with Piri Piri chilli.

Chicken65

This is South India's answer to the Chicken Tikka, hotter, redder and easier to make. Nobody really knows the origin of its name.

Butter Chicken

Invented by Moti Mahal restaurant in Delhi this started out as a way of rehydrating leftover Tandoori Chicken & tikka. Its the greatest Indian Curry of 20th century.
